# Korea Finance Rules

Use this as a planning reference for Korean residents. Treat policy details as variable unless the user provides current official data.

## Financial System Overview

Korean residents commonly use bank deposits, savings accounts, CMA or brokerage cash accounts, parking accounts, ISA, pension savings, IRP, workplace retirement pension, National Pension, housing subscription savings, insurance, and housing loans.

Personal finance planning should separate:

- Daily cash flow
- Emergency fund
- Debt payoff
- Short-term goals
- Long-term investments
- Pension and retirement
- Housing decisions
- Tax and insurance checks

## Deposit Protection

Deposit protection rules can change and vary by institution/product. Do not state a current limit as fact unless verified. Ask the user to confirm through KDIC, the institution, or official product documents.

## Cash Products

- Demand deposit: daily spending and transfer account.
- Parking account: flexible cash reserve, rate can change.
- Savings account/installment savings: goal-based accumulation.
- Time deposit: fixed term, early-withdrawal tradeoff.
- CMA: brokerage cash-management account; product structure and protection differ by type.

## Account Types

- ISA: tax-advantaged investment/savings wrapper. Contribution limits, eligibility, and tax benefits require current confirmation.
- Pension savings: long-term retirement account with tax-related features that require current confirmation.
- IRP: individual retirement pension; withdrawal restrictions and tax treatment require careful review.
- Workplace retirement pension: DB, DC, or IRP arrangement depending on employer and user choice.
- Housing subscription savings: used for cheongyak eligibility and housing planning; points and rules require current confirmation.

## National Pension

National Pension estimates should be based on official projections where possible. Contribution base, expected benefit, and eligibility rules can change.

## Tax-Aware Items

- Financial income comprehensive taxation can apply depending on interest/dividend income and current law.
- Overseas stock capital gains tax rules and basic deductions require current confirmation.
- Pension savings/IRP tax credits require current confirmation.
