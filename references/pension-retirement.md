# Pension And Retirement

Use this for retirement planning. Treat all benefit estimates and tax rules as assumptions unless verified from official statements.

## National Pension

Ask whether the user has an official National Pension estimate. If not, use a placeholder and mark it as unverified.

Check:

- Contribution history
- Expected benefit estimate
- Planned claiming age
- Employment/freelance/business status
- Spouse or dependent considerations

## Workplace Retirement Pension

Review whether the user has DB, DC, or IRP.

- DB: focus on employment stability, expected benefit formula, and employer plan.
- DC: focus on contribution amount, investment allocation, fees, and rebalancing.
- IRP: focus on retirement purpose, tax treatment, withdrawal restrictions, and fees.

## Pension Savings

Use for long-term retirement accumulation. Review contribution consistency, investment allocation, withdrawal restrictions, and tax treatment. Current tax-credit rules must be verified.

## Retirement Spending

Estimate with:

- Current monthly spending
- Housing status at retirement
- Health and insurance assumptions
- Family support obligations
- Inflation assumption
- Desired lifestyle

## Required Fund Estimate

Basic formula:

```text
annual retirement spending gap = expected annual spending - expected annual pension income
required retirement capital = annual retirement spending gap / assumed withdrawal rate
```

This is a planning estimate, not a guarantee.

## Scenario Returns

Use three scenarios if the user does not provide assumptions:

- Conservative: low real return assumption
- Base: moderate real return assumption
- Aggressive: higher return with higher volatility

Label all rates as assumptions.

## Cautions

- Early withdrawals can create tax and opportunity-cost issues.
- Pension receipt rules may affect tax.
- Inflation and medical costs can materially change retirement needs.
- Major decisions should be reviewed with qualified professionals.
