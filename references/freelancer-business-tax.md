# Freelancer And Business Tax

Use this for freelance or sole-proprietor checklists. It is not tax advice.

## Freelance Income

Ask for:

- Annual gross income estimate
- Payer count
- Withholding records
- Expense categories
- Work location and tools
- Side job vs main job

## 3.3 Percent Withholding

The withholding amount is not the final tax. The final result depends on total income, recognized expenses, deductions, credits, and filing status.

## Comprehensive Income Tax

Freelancers and mixed-income users may need comprehensive income tax filing. Ask for income by source and documented expenses.

## Business Registration

Do not tell the user that business registration is definitely required or unnecessary. Discuss factors:

- Revenue scale
- Repetition and continuity
- Client requirements
- VAT exposure
- Expense documentation
- Health insurance and pension impact

## Sole Proprietor Expenses

Common review areas:

- Equipment and software
- Office or studio costs
- Communication
- Transport
- Outsourcing
- Education directly tied to business
- Payment fees

Deductibility requires documentation and tax-rule confirmation.

## VAT

VAT obligations depend on business type, registration, revenue, and current rules. Mark as a tax-professional check item when uncertain.

## Health Insurance And National Pension

Income changes can affect health insurance premiums and National Pension status, especially for local subscribers or users leaving employment.

## Incorporation

Corporate conversion has tax, legal, payroll, accounting, and liability implications. Recommend professional consultation before suggesting it as a strategy.
