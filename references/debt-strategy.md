# Debt Strategy

Use this for debt payoff planning.

## Debt Types

- Credit loan
- Card loan
- Revolving credit
- Car installment
- Student loan
- Jeonse loan
- Mortgage
- Informal/family loan

## Data To Collect

For each debt:

- Balance
- Interest rate
- Minimum payment
- Remaining term
- Fixed or variable rate
- Collateral or guarantee
- Penalty or early repayment fee

## Payoff Methods

### Highest Interest First

Prioritize the debt with the highest effective rate after required minimum payments. This often reduces interest cost.

### Smallest Balance First

Prioritize the smallest balance after required minimum payments. This can improve motivation and simplify cash flow.

### Risk First

Prioritize delinquency prevention, revolving credit, card loans, and debts with severe penalties.

## Credit Score Management

General checklist:

- Avoid missed payments.
- Keep repayment records stable.
- Reduce revolving and high-utilization card debt.
- Avoid frequent unnecessary credit applications.

Do not guarantee score changes.

## Delinquency Risk

If the user cannot meet minimum payments:

- Stop optional spending changes from being the only plan.
- Encourage contacting lenders early.
- Review public or nonprofit support channels if relevant.
- Suggest qualified debt, legal, or counseling support.

Do not provide legal advice.
