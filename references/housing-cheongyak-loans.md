# Housing, Cheongyak, And Loans

Use this for housing decisions in Korea. Rules and eligibility change often; require current confirmation.

## Monthly Rent vs Jeonse vs Purchase

Compare:

- Upfront cash required
- Monthly cash-flow burden
- Loan payment
- Deposit safety and liquidity
- Moving cost
- Opportunity cost
- Reversibility
- Household stability and school/work needs

## Jeonse Loan Basics

Review:

- Deposit amount
- Loan amount
- Interest rate
- Monthly interest burden
- Guarantee/insurance requirements
- Contract and landlord risk
- Renewal risk

Loan availability and guarantee eligibility require financial-institution review.

## Mortgage Basics

Review:

- Purchase price
- Down payment
- Loan amount
- Interest rate type
- Term
- Monthly payment
- Maintenance, taxes, and transaction costs
- Stress if rates rise or income falls

## DSR, LTV, DTI

Use conceptually:

- DSR: debt-service burden compared with income.
- LTV: loan compared with property value.
- DTI: debt payment compared with income, often housing-loan focused.

Do not state current regulatory levels without verification.

## Cheongyak

Check:

- Housing subscription savings account
- Subscription period
- Household status
- Region
- Dependents
- Home ownership status
- Income/asset conditions if relevant
- Special supply categories if relevant

## Policy Programs To Verify

Depending on the user, check current conditions for:

- 생애최초
- 신혼부부
- 청년
- 디딤돌
- 버팀목
- 보금자리론 or similar policy loans

Always say eligibility and loan amount require current official and lender confirmation.
