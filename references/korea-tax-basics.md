# Korea Tax Basics

This reference supports checklist-level tax-aware planning only. It is not tax advice.

## Employee Year-End Settlement

For employees, review:

- Employment income withholding and year-end settlement documents
- Card spending, cash receipts, traditional market/public transport categories if relevant
- Medical, education, donation, insurance, rent, and housing-loan documents if relevant
- Pension savings and IRP contribution records
- Dependents and family changes

Eligibility, deduction rates, and limits require current official confirmation.

## Freelancers and 3.3 Percent Withholding

Freelancers often have income withheld at a simple rate before annual settlement. The actual comprehensive income tax result depends on total income, expenses, deductions, credits, and classification.

Check:

- Income statements by payer
- Withholding records
- Necessary expenses
- Health insurance and National Pension impact
- Whether business registration is appropriate

## Comprehensive Income Tax

Use for mixed income, freelance income, business income, rental income, financial income, and other taxable income categories. Filing outcome must not be guessed from one number.

## Sole Proprietor Basics

Review:

- Business registration status
- VAT obligations if applicable
- Income tax filing
- Necessary expense documentation
- Separate account and card use for business spending
- Simple vs standard expense treatment if relevant

## Necessary Expenses

Only business-related, documented, reasonable expenses should be considered. Do not tell the user a cost is definitely deductible without professional or official confirmation.

## Pension Savings and IRP

Pension savings and IRP may provide tax-credit benefits, but eligibility, limits, and withdrawal tax treatment require current confirmation.

## Financial Income

Interest and dividend income may trigger comprehensive taxation depending on current thresholds and the user's other income. Ask for annual interest/dividend estimates and mark the result as a check item.

## Overseas Stocks

Overseas stock capital gains, losses, exchange rates, and basic deductions require current confirmation. Do not state final tax due without transaction records and professional review.

## When To Suggest A Tax Professional

Recommend tax-professional review when the user has:

- Mixed employment and business/freelance income
- Large overseas stock sales
- High financial income
- Rental income
- Business expenses with weak documentation
- VAT obligations
- Family/dependent complexity
- Previous filing errors or notices
