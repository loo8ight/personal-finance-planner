# Investment Basics

Use this for allocation review and education. Do not provide individualized buy/sell instructions.

## Core Inputs

- Goal
- Time horizon
- Required liquidity
- Risk tolerance
- Current cash reserve
- Debt cost
- Asset allocation
- Account type

## Asset Categories

- Cash and deposits: stability and liquidity.
- Bonds or bond-like assets: income/stability, interest-rate risk.
- Domestic stocks/ETFs: equity risk and country concentration.
- Overseas stocks/ETFs: currency and global equity risk.
- Funds: manager/product/fee risk.
- Real estate: concentration, liquidity, leverage.
- Crypto/high-volatility assets: high drawdown and regulatory risk.

## Allocation Principles

- Match risk to time horizon.
- Keep emergency cash before taking avoidable risk.
- Diversify across asset classes and geographies when appropriate.
- Rebalance periodically instead of reacting to headlines.
- Watch tax, account, and fee differences.

## Installment Investing

Regular contributions can reduce timing stress but do not remove loss risk.

## Rebalancing

Review when:

- Allocation drifts materially.
- Goals or timeline change.
- Income stability changes.
- Debt cost changes.

## Required Warning

Past returns do not guarantee future returns. Individual securities, ETFs, funds, and cryptoassets should not be presented as must-buy or must-sell choices.
